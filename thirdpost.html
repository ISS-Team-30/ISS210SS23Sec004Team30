Climate Change Relation and Challenging Food Security

Posted by Hailey Boger- Climate Change Relation and Challenging Food Security on April 28, 2023

Climate change is causing more extreme weather events such as droughts and floods, which can damage crops and reduce their nutritional value.

It can also cause changes in temperature, precipitation patterns and rising carbon dioxide levels due to human activity, which can alter the timing of harvests and the way crops grow.

All of these factors can make it harder for farmers to produce high-quality, nutritious food.

As mentioned in the video, according to Kristie Ebi, when plants have higher carbon dioxide it increases the synthesis of carbohydrates, sugars and starches, and they decrease the concentrations of protein and critical nutrients.

These vitamins and nutrients are essential to our health and consuming plants that absorb these rising levels of CO2 puts us at risk for all kinds of health affects such as iron deficiency anemia and lack of protein.. These rising levels of CO2 have nearly doubled since the start of the industrial revolution and a solution for this would be to decrease greenhouse gases through the use of fossil fuels and adopting more sustainable diets such as less meat consumption could lead to a reduction in greenhouse gas emissions, which would overall lead to better health for you and our community.
