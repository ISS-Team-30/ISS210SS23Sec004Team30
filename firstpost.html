Food Quality and Income

How does income impact the quality of food that one eats?

Posted by Kennedy Singleton on April 28, 2023

There is a significant connection between low-quality foods and low-income households. Most fruits and vegetables, especially fresh good quality ones, are expensive and are sold at more luxury grocery stores. Additionally, lower-quality foods are usually mass produced, causing them to be cheaper and more accessible to those who have lower income. The article provided proved in a study that low income households were more likely to purchase sugary drinks, as well as fattier foods. This trend was exacerbated in households who were in the SNAP program or any other kind of government food assistance. Higher income households purchased more fruits and vegetables, as well as higher-quality snacks and foods.

The video provided shows an example of the implementation of a proposed solution to this problem. The previous article mentioned that SNAP and other food assistance programs should incentivise those who utilize their services to purchase more healthy foods. The video, about three years later, shows the United State Government working with Meijer to give discounts on fruits and vegetables to those who use SNAP.

The call-to-action in this situation would be further implementation of this type of program to more grocery stores throughout the world.
